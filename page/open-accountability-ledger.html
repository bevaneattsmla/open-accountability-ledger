The Open Accountability Ledger

We already paid for the answer.
Here's the receipt, and whether they acted on it.

Government commissions inquiries brilliantly and acts on them poorly. The answers are bought, filed, and lost, then a consultant is paid to rediscover them. This ledger is one public, permanent, machine-readable record of what government was told, what it promised, and what it did.

It is radical transparency pointed upward at power, not downward at the public. It holds recommendations, not people. It is the one part of this reform no one else has built, a database and a discipline, not a mega-project.

Build-phase demonstrator, internal, not for publication

First, the reform holds itself to its own standard.

A ledger that keeps score of everyone else has to keep score of itself. Before this asks a single agency to submit to the discipline, it submits first, every commitment public, with a status and a date, in the same format as every row below.

The guardrail, this is the opposite of Robodebt, on purpose

The ledger contains no personal data, runs no algorithm over any citizen, and makes no automated decision about anyone. Where Robodebt used automation and secrecy against individuals, this uses openness to hold government to promises it already made. Entries that are not yet verified to a primary source are shown greyed and flagged, the ledger's own honesty is part of the mechanism.
